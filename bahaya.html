The question is, how can methanol poison the body? The methanol that has entered the body will be metabolized by the enzyme alcohol dehydrogenase (DHA) to formaldehyde and then converted again into formic acid. Both substances are metabolic substances are harmful and toxic to the body, especially formic acid. In the case of methanol poisoning, formaldehyde is never detected in the victim's body fluids because the formaldehyde formed is rapidly converted to formic acid and subsequently takes a long time (approximately 20 hours) by a 10-formyl tetrahydrofolate synthetase (F-THF-S) to oxidize formic acid into carbon dioxide and water compounds so that there is a correlation between the concentration of formic acid in body fluids with the case of methanol poisoning.
